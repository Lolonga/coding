Geography and Map

Tanzania is 947,300 km² (365,754 mi²) and has a total coastline of 1,424 km (884.8 mi). Tanzania is engraved with national parks, with 22 parks about 15% of its land area is occupied by national parks. The relations with its neighbors are good as it is a major partner for Burundi, Rwanda, and DRC because it hosts the refugees The weather in Tanzania is a bit humid and comfortable It has 4 seasons long rainy,short rainy, dry season, and a hot season. Climate change has caused shorter rain seasons leading to fewer crops each year while rain season brings about an increase in waterborne countries.


Flag and History

According to Flag of Tanzania. The green section of the flag is a symbol of Tanzania's agriculture and its natural wonders, while the blue section represents its lakes, rivers, and the Indian Ocean. The black band represents the nation's people, and the yellow bands are symbols of the nation's mineral wealth. The flag was adopted on June 30, 1964. It was colonized by the British and became a sovereign entity on December 9 1961 On August 7 1998 nearly simultaneous bombs blew up in front of the American embassies in Nairobi, Kenya, and Dar es Salaam, Tanzania, the attacks were later connected to the al qaeda group.


People and culture

As of 2021 the population of Tanzania is 63.59 million of which 99% are of the Bantu ethnicity. Tanzania's population density of approximately 62 people per square kilometer The most heavily populated city is Dar Essalam Tanzanians have mostly Christian and Islamic holidays as most of the country is Christian or Islamic, The most notable holidays are Christmas, ramadan, Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha Tanzanian women wear “Kanzus” which are robesa with different colorful patterns.
